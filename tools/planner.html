What to ship, and when, to land Armenian goods in the EU on time.

Every cell is a shipment. The planner works backward from when EU shoppers buy — subtracting freight, customs and the Armenian harvest calendar — to tell you which month to commit each category. Read down a column for that month's order list; click a row for its story.

Select a category above to see its harvest window, lead time and month-by-month order line.
How to read the numbers. Volumes are in shipping cases using illustrative baselines — replace them with your real SKUs and forecasts and the whole schedule recomputes. Freight + customs lead time is modeled at ~2 months (1 for shelf-stable dry goods); the Armenian harvest calendar constrains when autumn-crop goods can physically ship. The demand curve reflects EU holiday gifting (December is the giant), Easter, and summer, weighted by diaspora demand. The point of the tool: it stops you ordering seasonal goods in the month you want to sell them.
